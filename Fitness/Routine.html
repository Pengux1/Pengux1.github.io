        3 sets, 6-8 reps

      • Seated Triceps Presssend

        Do a drop set on the last set by immediately reducing the weight by 20-30% and continuing the set.

        3 sets, 6-8 reps

      • Triceps Pushdownsend

        Do a drop set on the last set by immediately reducing the weight by 20-30% and continuing the set.

        3 sets, 6-8 reps

      • Barbell Curlsend

        To perform negatives use a weight that is about 20% more than your one-rep max and have a spotter help you through the positive portion of the rep. You should be able to slowly lower the negative rep for 3-5 seconds.

        3 sets, 3-5 reps

      • Barbell Curlsend

        Perform two rest-pauses on the last set by resting for 15 seconds after reaching muscle failure and continuing the set, then resting another 15 seconds after reaching muscle failure again, then continuing.

    • Monday - Legs / Calves

      From Here

      • Squat (Heavy Pyramid Set)send

        Increase the weight on each set until you peak at 8 reps, 4th set. If you want to go heaver you could do 6 reps for that set. You do not need to squat down too low, just above horizontal is fine. We don't want to hit the glutes to much.

        6 Sets, Reps: 15, 12, 10, 8, 10, 15

      • Stiff Leg Deadliftsend

        Really want to focus on stretch and contraction with these, not weight. If you're not feeling the stretch on the hamstrings on the way down and at the bottom you're not doing them right. Keep rep timing slow, 3-1-2 should be about right.

        4 Sets, 12 Reps

      • Leg Press / Leg Extension Supersetsend

        Again, we're going heavy on the leg press here, maximum 8 reps. Straight on to the leg extension after the set for 12 reps. Weight is not as important here. Make sure you squeeze at the top for a count of 3 seconds.

        4 Sets, Reps: 8 press / 12 extension

      • Leg Curlsend

        Heavy again with leg curls. Heavy, but controlled. You should still be able to manage the weight. Use 2-1-2 rep timing.

        4 Sets, 8 Reps

    • Thursday - Chest / Shoulders / Traps

      From Here

      • Barbell Bench Presssend

        Make sure you lower the bar slowly for four seconds before pushing the weight back up.

        Lie back on a flat bench holding a barbell in the rack above you with a shoulder-width, overhand grip. Lift the bar off the rack and position it above your chest with arms fully extended. From the starting position, breathe in and lower the bar slowly until it skims the middle of your chest. Push the bar back to the starting position explosively as you breathe out. That’s one rep.

        3 Sets, 6 Reps

      • Incline Dumbbell Presssend

        Lie back on a bench set to a 30-degree angle and lift the weights up to shoulder height, palms facing away from you. Breathe out as you press up with both arms. Lock out your arms and squeeze your chest before returning slowly to the start position.

        3 Sets, 12 Reps

      • Cable Pec Flysend

        Set the pulley as low as it can go on the cable machine to target the lower-portion of your chest.

        Attach stirrup handles to the high pulleys of a cable crossover machine. Take one in each hand – your arms should be outstretched with a slight bend at the elbow. Place one foot slightly forward, brace you core, and pull the handles slightly downward and across your body until your hands meet, then return to the start position.

        3 Sets, 25 Reps

      • Barbell Military Presssend

        Position your feet close together and lift the barbell up to your shoulders, palms facing forward. Press the barbell above your head explosively until your arms are fully extended, then lower the weight under control.

        3 Sets, 6 Reps

      • One Arm Kettlebell Arnold Presssend

        Hold a kettlebell at shoulder height, palm facing inwards and arm bent. Rotate your palm and extend your arm to raise the kettlebell above your head. Pause, then repeat.

        3 Setcs, 12 Reps

      • Lateral Raisesend

        Pick a couple of dumbbells and stand with them by your sides, palms facing your body. Keeping your upper body still – that means no swinging – lift the dumbbells out to your side with a slight bend at your elbows. Lift until your arms are parallel to the floor then slowly lower to the start position.

        3 Setcs, 25 Reps

      • Dipssend

        Make sure you lower your body slowly for four seconds before pushing back up.

        Grab the bars of a dip station with your palms facing inward and your arms straight. Slowly lower until your elbows are at right angles, ensuring they stay tucked against your body and don't flare out. Drive yourself back up to the top and repeat.

        3 Setcs, 6 Reps

      • Close-Grip Bench Presssend

        Lie back on a flat bench holding a barbell with a narrow, overhand grip. From the starting position, breathe in and lower the bar slowly until it skims the middle of your chest. Push the bar back to the starting position explosively as you breathe out. Focus on pushing the bar using your chest muscles.

        3 Sets, 12 Reps
