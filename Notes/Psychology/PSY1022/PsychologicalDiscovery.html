Learning Objectives

  • Explain the importance of studying research design, methods, and analysis.
  • Differentiate the scientific method from other sources of knowledge, and describe the goals of science.
  • Provide an outline of the steps involved in the research process.
  • Give examples of possible sources of research ideas.
  • Generate research hypotheses, and distinguish between hypotheses and theories.

  • Summarize the main ethical principles that guide psychological research, and strategies for conducting research in an ethical manner.
  • Define what a variable is, and distinguish between dependent, independent, and extraneous variables.
  • Develop an operational definition.
  • Give examples of nominal, ordinal, interval, and ratio scales of measurement, and differentiate between them.
  • Discuss and distinguish between measurement reliability and validity.

Learning Objectives

  • Differentiate between populations and samples, and explain the importance of obtaining representative samples.
  • Provide examples of different sampling techniques, and comment on the likelihood that they will result in representative samples.
  • Distinguish between qualitative and quantitative research.
  • Describe the goal of descriptive methods (namely, observational, case study, and survey methods), as well as discuss the advantages and disadvantages of these methods.
  • Describe the goal of the correlational method, as well as its advantages and disadvantages (including the directionality problem and third variable problem).

Learning Objectives

  • Describe the goal of the experimental research method, as well as discuss its advantages and disadvantages.
  • Differentiate between experimental and quasi-experimental research.
  • Explain how manipulation and control distinguish the experimental method from other research strategies, and provide examples of techniques for controlling extraneous variables.
  • Summarise the advantages and disadvantages of using between-, within-, and matched-subjects designs.
  • Discuss how the concepts of internal and external validity apply to the various research strategies.

Learning Objectives

  • Distinguish between descriptive and inferential statistics.
  • Organise and summarise data in the form of frequency distribution tables and graphs.
  • Calculate and differentiate between the three measures of central tendency (mean, median, and mode).
  • Describe and calculate measures of variability or dispersion (range, variance, standard deviation).
  • Describe the position of a particular score in a distribution using t-scores.
  • Summarise the properties of the standard normal distribution and its relationship with probability.

Learning Objectives

  • Describe the position of a sample mean using t-scores, the sampling distribution of means, and Central Limit Theorem.
  • Determine how representative a sample mean is using hypothesis testing (i.e., t-test).
  • Summarise the steps involved in null hypothesis significance testing.
  • Outline the possible errors associated with null hypothesis significance testing.
  • Discuss how power and effect size relate to null hypothesis significance testing.
