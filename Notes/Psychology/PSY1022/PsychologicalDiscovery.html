Learning Objectives

  • Explain the importance of studying research design, methods, and analysis.
  • Differentiate the scientific method from other sources of knowledge, and describe the goals of science.
  • Provide an outline of the steps involved in the research process.
  • Give examples of possible sources of research ideas.
  • Generate research hypotheses, and distinguish between hypotheses and theories.

Learning Objectives

  • Summarize the main ethical principles that guide psychological research, and strategies for conducting research in an ethical manner.
  • Define what a variable is, and distinguish between dependent, independent, and extraneous variables.
  • Develop an operational definition.
  • Give examples of nominal, ordinal, interval, and ratio scales of measurement, and differentiate between them.
  • Discuss and distinguish between measurement reliability and validity.
