Learning Objectives

  • Identify the ways that memories do and do not accurately reflect experiences.
  • Explain the function, span and duration of each of the three memory systems.
  • Differentiate the sub-types of long-term memory.
  • Identify methods for connecting new information to existing knowledge (memories).
  • Explain how mnemonic strategies can improve memory.
  • Identify the role that schemas play in the storage of memories.
  • Distinguish ways of measuring memory.
  • Describe how the relation between encoding and retrieval conditions influences remembering.
  • Identify reasons why people forget.
