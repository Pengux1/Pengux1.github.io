Learning Objectives

  • Identify criteria for defining mental disorders.
  • Describe conceptions of diagnoses across history and cultures.
  • Identify common misconceptions about psychiatric diagnoses, and the strengths and limitations of the current diagnostic system.
  • Identify the characteristics of different mood disorders.
  • Describe major explanations for depression, and how life events can interact with characteristics of the individual to produce depressive symptoms.
  • Identify common myths and misconceptions about suicide.
  • Recognise the characteristic symptoms of schizophrenia.
  • Explain how psychosocial, neural, biochemical and genetic influences create the vulnerability to schizophrenia.

Learning Objectives

  • Describe the many ways people experience anxiety.
  • Describe the characteristics of people who seek treatment, benefit from psychotherapy, and practise psychotherapy.
  • Distinguish between professionals and paraprofessionals, and describe what it takes to be an effective therapist.
  • Describe the core beliefs and criticisms of psychodynamic therapies.
  • Describe and evaluate the effectiveness of humanistic therapies.
  • Identify the advantages of group methods.
  • Describe the research evidence concerning the effectiveness of Alcoholics Annonymous.
  • Identify different approaches to treating the dysfunctional family system.
  • Describe the characteristics of behaviour therapy, and identify different behavioural approaches.
  • Describe the features of cognitive-behavioural therapies (CBT) and third wave therapies.
  • Describe how prevention strategies can help manage mental health conditions.
  • Evaluate the claim that all psychotherapies are equally effective.
  • Explain how ineffective therapies can sometimes appear to be effective.
  • Explain the importance of evaluating treatment effectiveness.
  • Identify methods to evaluate treatment efficacy.
  • Recognise different types of drugs and cautions associated with drug treatment.
  • Outline key considerations in drug treatment.
  • Identify misconceptions about biomedical treatments.
