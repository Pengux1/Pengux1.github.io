Physical Attractiveness

Matching Hypothesis - People tend to form relationships with those who are similar to themselves in physical attractiveness.

Evolutionary Perspective

What we find attractive is pre-historically determined. So women prefer to mate with men who would be good fathers and be good prociders; while men prefer to mate with women who could bear healthy babies.

Intimate Relationships and Love

Interdependence - Each partner's thoughts, actions and behaviour affects the other.

Commitment - Psychological attachment to the relationship; involves trust, honesty, etc.

Co-Dependent Relationship - They put aside their own well-being to maintain a relationship with another.

Independent Relationship - Both remain independent in the relationship?

Sternberg's Triangular Theory of Love

Three basic components - Passion, Intimacy, Commitment.

  • With their powers combined, they can form:

  • Consummate love: Passion + Intimacy + Commitment (long term partnership based on a blissful connection between partners).
  • Companionate love: Intimacy + Commitment (mellow phase in a relationship).
  • Romantic love: Passion + Intimacy (more focused on ‘now’ rather than the future).
  • Fatuous love: Commitment + Passion (e.g., many celebrity marriages).

Asch's Conformity Experiment

A participant walks into a room with people seated around the table, who unbeknownst to the participant are paid actors. They are then presented with a piece of paper with three lines on it, one visibly larger than the others. They are then, one by one, asked which line they think is bigger. The paid actors however, gave the wrong answer.

So basically, a disturbing amount of people conformed with the actors answers, and gave the wrong answer.

When do People Conform?

Ambiguity of situation, Unanimity and size of majority (Social Impact Theory), Gender, other members of the group are higher in social status.

Conformity decreases when people are able to respond privately.

Why do people conform?

Why not?

Two types of conformity - Public and Private.

Public occurs when people truly believe that the group is right.

Private occurs when we are pressured and feel we do not have a choice other than to conform to group norms.

Compliance

Change due to a request.

Foot-in-the-door technique - First make a small request, and then move to a bigger one; will only work if the bigger request is consistent with the smaller one.

Door-in-the-face technique - Start with a big request, and then make a smaller one; will work only if the same person makes the request which is consistent with the initial one.

Low-ball technique - First make a person agree, and then increase your terms; works due to commitment as at first the person has already said ‘yes’.

So an actor is in a room with fake electrodes attached to them. They were instructed to give an electric shock every time the actor got a question wrong, and to increase the voltage each time. Deindividuation

The tendency of people to engage in atypical behaviour when stripped of their usual identities (Feeling of anonymity and lack of individual responsibility).

When this happens we become more vulnerable to social influences (Conformity and Obedience) and Social Roles (Very scary).

When this happens we risk losing our typical identities and become irrational.

Types of Agression

Hostile / Impulsive Aggression - Violent attitudes or actions, behaviour that cannot be controlled and aimed at hurting others.

Instrumental Aggression - Aggressive behaviour to achieve a goal, means to a goal.

Why do people help?

Personaity of the helper: Empathy.

Situational Influences - characteristics of the victim, no easy escape, exposure to helpful role models, mood, being in a hurry.

Enlightenment Effect - public knowledge about psychological research can change the world.

Cost-Reward Theory

Helping reduces the unpleasant arousal felt when confronted with suffering.

3 stages: arousal, labelling, evaluation of outcomes.

Evaluate the consequences of helping/not helping.

Chooses the action with lowest costs = reduces personal distress.

Empathy costs (feeling of stress – not helping).

Personal Costs - Blame, and even penance when it is legally clear that a bystander failed to do his or her civic duty - when there are a lot of other bystanders, these last costs are less and therefore there are fewer reasons to help.

Bystander Effect

Diffusion of Responsibility - The presence of others make each person feel less responsible for the outcomes.

Pluralistic Ignorance - Assuming that no one in the group perceives things as we do.

Social Loafing - People working in groups feel less responsible for the outcomes of a project than they do when working alone.
