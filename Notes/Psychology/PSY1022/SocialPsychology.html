Outline theories of attribution – Fundamental attribution error and discuss sources of attributions.


Attributions

Attributions are your attempt at understanding your experiences, behaviours, and the behaviour of others.

Internal Attribution is behaviour explained due to personal factors like traits, attitudes, feelings, etc.

External Attribution is behaviour explained due to situational factors.

The Fundamental Attribution Error

This is defined as: The tendency to overestimate the the contribution of lasting traits and dispositions in determining people's behavior, as compared to situational effects. We might see someone kicking a vending machine, and conclude they're an inherently angry person. But maybe they just failed a test, had their driving license revoked, and had the machine eat their money for the third time this week. We think of the other person as an evil mutant and ourselves as righteous actors.

Basically, we assume other peoples actions are because of their personality rather than because of their personal situation.

Actor-Observer Effect

A tendency to attribute one's own actions to external causes while attributing other people's behaviors to internal causes. It is a type of attributional bias that plays a role in how we perceive and interact with other people.

Prejudice

Stereotype - Shortcut: A belief about a group’s characteristic that we apply to most members of that group.

Seed of prejudice -> Ultimate Attribution Error -> Attributing the negative behaviour of entire groups to their dispositions.

Prejudice - Negative Attitudes - Pre-judging

Adaptive conservatism

In-group bias: Tendency to favour individuals inside our group relative to members outside our group

Out-group homogeneity: Tendency to view all people outside our group as highly similar -> easy to dismiss.

Theories of Prejudice

Motivational Theories - Motivational Theories - Authoritarianism personality trait. Acceptance of traditional values, blindly follow orders from authority, be prejudiced against others who are different.

Cognitive Theories - The way in which people deal with the world - use of schemas - helps us draw proper conclusions about people, but can sometimes be wrong.

Learning Theories - Prejudice is learned through conflicts, observing, listening to others, biopreparedness - natural fear of strangers who are different to us.

Sternberg's Triangular Theory of Love

Three basic components - Passion, Intimacy, Commitment.

  • With their powers combined, they can form:

  • Consummate love: Passion + Intimacy + Commitment (long term partnership based on a blissful connection between partners).
  • Companionate love: Intimacy + Commitment (mellow phase in a relationship).
  • Romantic love: Passion + Intimacy (more focused on ‘now’ rather than the future).
  • Fatuous love: Commitment + Passion (e.g., many celebrity marriages).

Two types of conformity - Public and Private.

Public occurs when people truly believe that the group is right.

Compliance

Change due to a request.

Foot-in-the-door technique - First make a small request, and then move to a bigger one; will only work if the bigger request is consistent with the smaller one.

Door-in-the-face technique - Start with a big request, and then make a smaller one; will work only if the same person makes the request which is consistent with the initial one.

Low-ball technique - First make a person agree, and then increase your terms; works due to commitment as at first the person has already said ‘yes’.
