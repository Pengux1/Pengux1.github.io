Homeostasis - Salt and Water Balance

Learning Objectives

  • Give a concise account of the role of excretory systems in general.
  • Explain the difference between osmoconformers, ionic regulators, hypertonic and hypotonic osmoregulators.
  • Distinguish between animals that excrete ammonia, urea and urate.
  • Describe the challenges of water balance to animals in freshwater and marine environments.
  • Describe the avenues of water gain and loss in terrestrial animals.
  • Describe the difference between a protonephridium and a metanephridium.
  • Describe the excretory system of insects.
  • Detail the structure and function of the mammalian nephron.
  • Give a brief overview of the evolution of the vertebrate nephron.
  • Explain the structure and function of the mammalian kidney.
  • Explain how the two-solute model enables formation of a concentration gradient in the kidney.
  • Identify the regions of active transport and passive transport of ions and water in the nephron.
