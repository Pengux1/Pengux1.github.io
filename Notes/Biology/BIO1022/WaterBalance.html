Give a concise account of the role of excretory systems in general.

Role of the Excretory System

The Excretory System is the system responible for the the bodily process of discharging wastes.

So the wastes produced by the normal functioning of the body, as well as any toxins that might have been consumed (Cough Cough Alcohol) need to get out of the body before they can cause damage. To do this, the excretory system does a variety of actions that filter out the toxins, and expell it from the body.

Explain the difference between osmoconformers, ionic regulators, hypertonic and hypotonic osmoregulators.

Osmoconformers

Osmoconformers are organisms (Such as Freshwater Fish) that match their body osmolarity to their enviroment actively or passively. Most marine invertebrates are osmoconformers, but their ionic composition may be different from that of seawater.

Ionic Regulators

Ionic Regulators don't appear to exist according to Google. The best I can find is a quizlet which states: "Maintaince of constant ion concentration in blood plasma."

Hypertonic and Hypotonic Osmoregulators

Osmoregulators tightly regulate their body osmolarity (Such as salt concentrations) to stay constant, despite their external enviroment.

Freshwater Fish are an example of Hypertonic Osmoregulators, whose gills actively uptake salt from the enviroment, Water will dfiffuse into the fish so it excretes a very hypotonic urine to expel the excess water.

Marine Fish are an example of Hypotonic Osmoregulators, whose internal osmotic concentration is lower than the surrounding seawater, which means it tends to lose water and gain salt. It actively excretes salt out of the gills to maintain osmolarity.

Distinguish between animals that excrete ammonia, urea and urate.

Animals That Excrete Ammonoia

Ammonia (NH3) is produced as a biproduct of regular metabolism in most animals, but is very toxic to tissues and extremely soluble in water. A large amount of water is needed for the excretion of Ammonia as well, about 0.5L per 1g of nitrogen.

Therefore, this is mainly done by marine animals who can excrete ammonia directly into the water. An animal that excretes Ammonia is called ammonotelic.
