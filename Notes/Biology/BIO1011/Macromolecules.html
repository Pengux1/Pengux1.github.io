Macromolecules


Learning Objectives

  • Lecture One
    • Understand the hierarchy of structural and functional levels in biology.
    • Understand the properties associated with life.
    • Understand the concept of scale in biological systems - from organelle to cell to tissue to organ.
    • Distinguish between different forms of bonding.
    • Understand the structure and geometry of a water molecule.
    • Explain the relationship between the polar nature of water and its ability to form hydrogen bonds.
  • Lecture Two
    • Distinguish between monomers that make up macromolecules, and the bonds that link monomers.
    • Define a nucleotide and give examples of biologically important nucleotides.
    • Provide examples of mono- and disaccharides.
    • Give examples of important structural and storage polysaccharides.
    • Understand the roles of proteins in organisms.
    • Understand the different levels of structure in proteins.
    • Define classes of lipids and understand their functions and biological importance.
