Group 6 Elements

d-block metals have clear trends that you need to be familiar with. All d-block elements are metals, they all form compounds in a variety of oxidation states, form coloured compounds and exhibit magnetic properties.

From this table you can see that there is an increase in atomic radius down the group from Cr to Mo, but the radii of Mo and W are the same. This is due to Lanthanide Contraction, which refers to the fact that the 5s and 5p orbitals penetrate the 4f sub-shell so the 4f orbital is not shielded from the increasing nuclear change, which causes the atomic radius of the atom to decrease. This decrease in size continues throughout the series.
