Structure of the Atom


Learning Objectives

  • Understand the structure of the atom and important discoveries that revealed the existence of protons, electrons and neutrons.
  • Review the terms atomic number and mass number.
  • Recognize light as one form of electromagnetic radiation.
  • Understand the meaning of the terms frequency and wavelength, and their inter-relationship νλ = c.
  • Investigate the concept of “wave-particle duality”, as revealed through the profound ideas and experiments of Planck, Einstein, Bohr, DeBroglie, Heisenberg and Schrodinger.
  • To see that light has both wave and particle-like properties (hence photons) and that the same is true for electrons.
  • To understand the need for a new “quantum mechanics” to describe the behaviour of electrons in an atom; this replaced the “classical mechanics” used since the time of Newton.
  • Discuss the use of the four quantum numbers.
  • Define an atomic orbital & discuss the different shapes and features.
  • Explore the concepts of penetration and shielding & explain the origin of non-degeneracy between the orbitals of many electron atoms.

Dalton

Atoms are the smallest particles of an element that retain the chemical identity of that element; atoms of different elements have different properties.

J.J. Thomson

In 1897 J.J. Thomson discovers “cathode rays” in a discharge tube – these rays are a stream of negatively charged particles called electrons. Thomson suddenly measured their charge-to-mass ratio.

In 1909 Millikan directly measures the electron’s charge.

Rutherford

Rutherford performs a classic experiment in which he fires a beam of α particles at a thin gold foil - most pass straight through but some are back-scattered!

Rutherford’s conclusion: Most of the mass of the Au atoms and +ve charge resides in an extremely tiny dense “nucleus” – most of the total volume of the atom is empty spaaaaaace in which the electrons move.



Review the terms atomic number and mass number.


Atomic Number

The number of protons in the nucleus of an atom, which is characteristic of a chemical element and determines its place in the periodic table.

So literally how many protons an element has.

Mass Number

The total number of protons and neutrons in a nucleus.

Electrons aren't included in this, as they are so small they have almost no effect on the mass of an atom. Protons and Neutrons have almost the same mass, and are counted as equivilant.

Understand the meaning of the terms frequency and wavelength, and their inter-relationship νλ = c.


Frequency

The number of waves passing though origin point per unit of time.

Represented by ν in Hz or s^-1.

Wavelength

The distance from peak to peak.

Represented by λ in meters, m.

Amplitude

The difference between the height of the peak and the origin.

Relationship between Frequency and Wavelength

νλ = c

Where ν is Freqeuency, λ is Wavelength, and c is the speed of light (3.00*10^8).
